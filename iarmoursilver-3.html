❓ How do I change a nominee?▼

Only you can change your nominee — not your family, even with everyone's agreement. You do it directly with each bank, insurer, or institution.

How: Visit the bank/branch (or use net-banking) and fill a nominee change form — banks usually call this Form DA1. You'll need the new nominee's name, relationship to you, and their ID proof (Aadhaar/PAN). Your signature (or thumb impression + witness) is required.

Important: A nominee only collects the money first — it doesn't legally make them the owner. Your other children can still claim their share under inheritance law unless you also have a will. If you want one child to truly inherit more, a will is the stronger tool — nomination alone won't do that.

💡 For the exact form your bank uses, or for will/inheritance questions, it's best to confirm with your bank's nomination desk or a local lawyer.
